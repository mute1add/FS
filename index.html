Build a healthier, smoother and softer skin

Microrelief is defined as cutaneous microstructure resembling irregular furrows on the skin surface. Microrelief pattern on skin has been widely used to evaluate skin health and aging. To evaluate the effect of
Brightum* on skin microrelief, a clinical study of 20 volunteers aged from
35 to 65 was conducted. Cream containing with/without Brightum was applied twice a day for 28 days. At the beginning and end of this study,
skin texture was evaluated by the profilometry.

The results indicate that Brightum* decreases the skin’s microrelief,
-6.3% on average and up to -42.9%. These data suggest the role of
Brightum in smoothing and softening the skin texture.

Brightum clinically decrease microrelief

Enhance skin clarity and luminosity by targeting pigment cells

Irregular accumulating of melanin within the epidermis can cause uneven tones and skin pigmentation. Melanin is produced by melanocyte, a pigment cell within epidermis. Within melanocytes, an enzyme called tyrosinase utilizes tyrosine to generate pigment melanin. To evaluate the effect of Brightum*** on melanin production, an in-vitro assay was generated to mimic the process which tyrosinase utilizes tyrosine to generate melanin, plus Brightum could be added. The results were obtained by quantifying melanin levels.

The results indicate that Brightum*** significantly reduces melanin
production. These data also suggest the potential role of Brightum in
mediating melanocyte to produce less pigment melanin.

Brightum significantly reduce melanin production

To further evaluate the effect of Brightum*** on human skin tones, a
clinical study of 24 volunteers were conducted. Cream containing with/without Brightum was applied twice a day for 28 days. At the beginning and end of this study, the color of skin was measured by a chromameter. The results were analyzed by using skin color topology.
Data was summarized by using typology angles generated
from volunteers.

The results indicate that Brightum*** significantly increases skin clarity
and decreases skin pigmentation.

Brightum enhances skin fibroblast proliferation

Reprogram dermal fibroblasts to enhance collagen production

Collagen loss can cause fine lines and wrinkles. The loss of collagen can also reduce skin elasticity. To evaluate the effect of Brightum** on collagen production, in-vitro studies were performed. Human dermal fibroblasts from healthy donners were cultured with/without Brightum for 48 hours. At the end of the study, collagen synthesis was evaluated.

The results indicate that Brightum** enhances type I collagen synthesis of
human dermal fibroblasts.

Brightum stimulates Type I collagen production

To further evaluate the effect of Brightum** in a clinical setting, 42 volunteers with wrinkles in the crow’s feet area were recruited to use cream with/without Brightum twice a day and total for 28 days. At the beginning and the end of the study, the depth of crow’s feet wrinkles was evaluated by skin profilometry.

The results indicate that Brightum** decreases the wrinkle depth of
human skin, as high as 9.1% average in comparison with the control. **p˂0.01

Brightum clinically improves winkle depth
